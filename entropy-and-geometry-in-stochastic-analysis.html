Entropy and geometry in stochastic analysis

Entropy, Curvature, and Functional Inequalities in Stochastic Analysis

FirstName LASTNAME Affiliation
FirstName LASTNAME Affiliation

Entropy methods have played a central role in stochastic analysis, particularly in the study of Markov diffusion processes, optimal transport, and functional inequalities. In this talk, we explore the interplay between entropy, Ricci curvature, and stochastic processes, focusing on the geometric structures underlying probability measures and their evolution.

We analyze entropy functionals associated with stochastic differential equations (SDEs) and their connection to Wasserstein geometry. Specifically, we investigate the behavior of the entropy along solutions to stochastic evolution equations and establish new functional inequalities linking entropy, curvature, and mixing properties of diffusions.

Theorem Let \((M,g)\) be a complete Riemannian manifold with Ricci curvature bounded below by \(K > 0\). Consider a diffusion process \((X_t)_{t\geq0}\) on \(M\) governed by the generator \(L = \Delta - \nabla V \cdot \nabla\). Then, for the associated probability measure \(\mu_t\) evolving according to the Fokker-Planck equation, we have the following entropy dissipation inequality: \[\tag{1} \frac{d}{dt} H(\mu_t | \mu_\infty) \leq - 2K H(\mu_t | \mu_\infty),\] where \(H(\mu_t | \mu_\infty)\) denotes the relative entropy with respect to the invariant measure \(\mu_\infty\).

Corollary Under the same conditions, the measure \(\mu_t\) converges exponentially fast to \(\mu_\infty\) in relative entropy: \[\tag{2} H(\mu_t | \mu_\infty) \leq H(\mu_0 | \mu_\infty) e^{-2Kt}.\] This result provides an explicit entropy-based convergence rate for diffusion processes in positively curved spaces.

These findings have implications for gradient flow techniques, large deviations, and information geometry, offering a deeper understanding of the role of entropy in stochastic analysis.
