Invited session 1

Large environment

Dariusz BURACZEWSKI University of Wrocław, Poland
Piotr DYSZEWSKI University of Wrocław, Poland, E-mail: pdysz@math.uni.wroc.pl

We will concentrate on a population growth process in which the reproduction law is randomly picked at each generation. Denote by \(\mathcal{Q}=\{ Q_n \}_{n \geq 0}\) a sequence of iid random measures on the set of nonegative integers \(\mathbb{N}\). The sequence \(\{Z_n\}_{n \geq 0}\) is called a branching process in random environment if \(Z_0 =1\), and \[\tag{1} Z_{n+1} {=} \sum_{k=1}^{Z_n} \xi^n_k,\] where given \(\mathcal{Q}\), \(\xi^n_k\) are iid and independent from \(Z_n\) with common distribution \(Q_n\). Our first goal is to establish a precise large deviation estimates, i. e. to find a precise asymptotic of \[\mathbb{P}[Z_n > e^{\rho n}] \quad \mbox{as} \quad n \to \infty,\] where \(\rho >0\) is a fixed parameter. Further investigation of our techniques reveals that apart form knowing the probability of large deviations of \(\{Z_n\}_{n \geq 0}\), we can also point out the moment when it arises. More precisely, we will consider the exceedance time (or first passage time) for \(\{Z_n\}_{n\geq 0}\) defined viz. \[T_t = \inf \{ n \geq 0 \: | \: Z_n>t \}.\] We will study the large deviations of \(\{T_t\}_{t \geq 0}\) and describe the probability that \(\{Z_n\}_{n \geq 0}\) exceeds some threshold \(t\) precisely at some given moment, that is we will find the asymptotic of \[\mathbb{P}\left[T_t = \left\lfloor\frac{\log(t)}{\rho} \right\rfloor\right] \quad \mbox{as} \quad t \to \infty,\] where again \(\rho >0\) is some fixed parameter.
