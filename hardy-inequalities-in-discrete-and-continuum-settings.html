Hardy Inequalities in Discrete and Continuum Settings

Hardy (in)equalities for semigroups

Krzysztof BOGDAN Wrocław University of Science and Technology
Krzysztof BOGDAN Wrocław University of Science and Technology

The purpose of the talk is to point out the connection of superharmonic functions for Hardy-type inequalities for Sobolev-Bregman forms, or \(p\)-forms, where \(1<p<\infty\). The guiding principle is that for a Dirichlet form \(\mathcal E\) on \(L^2(X,dx)\) with generator \(\mathcal L\) and (nonnegative, superharmonic) function \(h>0\), \(\mathcal L h\le 0\), we have \[\mathcal E(u,u)\ge \int \frac{-\mathcal L h}{h} u^2\] for functions \(u\) in the domain of \(\mathcal E\), see \[1\]. More specifically, we consider a (symmetric) transition probability density \[p_t(x,y)=p_t(y,x), \quad x,y\in X,\; t>0,\] (nonnegative) increasing function \(f:[0,\infty)\to [0,\infty),\) a measure \(\mu\) on \(X\), and define \[h(x):=\int_0^\infty f(s)P_s\mu(x)ds, \quad x\in X.\] This function is superharmonic, in fact, excessive for the semigroup \(P_tu(x):=\int_X p_t(x,y)u(y)dy\). If we denote \[q(x):= \frac{1}{h(x)}\int_0^\infty f'(s)P_s\mu(x)ds,\] then, guided by the above principle, we get the Hardy inequality \[\tag{1} \mathcal E(u,u)\ge \int_X u(x)^2 q(x)dx,\] We will use the function \(h\) to condition in a sense of Doob the following Sobolev-Bregman form of the fractional Laplacian $$ \[\begin{aligned} \mathcal E_p [u] &:=& \frac{1}{2} \int_{\mathbb{R}^d}\int_{\mathbb{R}^d} (u(y)-u(x)) \left( u(y)^{\langle p - 1 \rangle} - u(x)^{\langle p - 1 \rangle} \right) \nu(x,y)\,dxdy, \end{aligned}\]

\[ where \](x,y)=2{}((d+)/2){-d/2}/|(-/2)||y-x|^{-d-}, x, y ,$$ In \[4\], we give a ground-state representation for \(\mathcal E_p[u]\), which extends the Hardy identities from \[2\] and \[3, Proposition 4.1\]. We then get the following optimal Hardy inequality.

Theorem 1. Let \(0<\alpha<d \wedge 2\), \(0 \leq \beta \leq d-\alpha\), and \(1<p<\infty\). Then, \[\begin{aligned} \mathcal E_p{[u]}&\geq \kappa_{(d-\alpha)/p} \int_{{\mathbb{R}^d}} \frac{|u(x)|^p}{|x|^{\alpha}} \,dx \quad \text{ for all } \quad u \in L^p({\mathbb{R}^d}), \label{e.Hp} \end{aligned}\] where \[\label{e.dkb} \kappa_{\beta}:=\frac{2^\alpha\Gamma\big(\frac{\beta+\alpha}{2}\big) \Gamma\big(\frac{d-\beta}{2}\big)}{\Gamma\big(\frac{\beta}{2}\big)\Gamma\big(\frac{d-\beta-\alpha}{2}\big)}.\]

It is important to notice that \[e.Hp\] improves the Hardy inequality transferred from \(L^2\), namely for \(p\neq 2\), we have \[\label{e.i} \kappa_{(d-\alpha)/p}>\frac{4(p-1)}{p^2} \, \frac{2^\alpha\Gamma\left(\frac{d+\alpha}{4}\right)^2} {\Gamma\left(\frac{d-\alpha}{4}\right)^{2}},\] see Figure 1.

Illustration of \[e.i\] for \(d=3, \alpha=1\)

We also give applications to contractivity of Schrödinger perturbations of the fractional Laplacian. The above results are given in \[4\].
