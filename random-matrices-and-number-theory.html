Random Matrix Models and their Applications in Number Theory

Tags: Random Matrices, Number Theory

FirstName LASTNAME Affiliation
FirstName LASTNAME Affiliation

Random matrix theory (RMT) has emerged as a powerful tool in number theory, providing deep insights into the statistical behavior of zeta functions, L-functions, and arithmetic properties of primes. In this talk, we will explore recent developments in the interplay between random matrices and number theory, focusing on universality properties, eigenvalue distributions, and connections to the Riemann zeta function.

One of the central themes of this discussion is the remarkable analogy between the eigenvalue statistics of random matrices from classical compact groups and the zeros of the Riemann zeta function. We present new results on the correlation functions of eigenvalues in ensembles such as the Gaussian Unitary Ensemble (GUE) and their implications for the distribution of L-function zeros.

Theorem Let \(H_N\) be an \(N \times N\) random Hermitian matrix drawn from the GUE. Then, the limiting distribution of eigenvalues follows the Wigner semicircle law: \[\tag{1} \rho_N(\lambda) = \frac{1}{\pi} \sqrt{2N - \lambda^2}, \quad |\lambda| \leq \sqrt{2N}.\] Moreover, under appropriate scaling, the local eigenvalue statistics converge to the sine-kernel distribution observed in the zeros of the Riemann zeta function on the critical line.

Corollary The pair correlation function of the normalized zeros of the Riemann zeta function matches that of GUE eigenvalues: \[\tag{2} R_2(s) = 1 - \frac{\sin^2(\pi s)}{(\pi s)^2},\] indicating strong statistical similarities between random matrices and number-theoretic objects.

These results provide further evidence for the conjectured link between RMT and the distribution of prime numbers, with applications extending to quantum chaos and mathematical physics.

Moments of L-Functions and Random Matrix Models

Tags: Random Matrices

FirstName LASTNAME Affiliation

The study of moments of L-functions is a central problem in analytic number theory, closely related to the distribution of prime numbers and deep conjectures such as the Riemann Hypothesis. In recent years, random matrix theory (RMT) has provided compelling models for predicting the asymptotic behavior of these moments. In this talk, we explore new results on the connection between RMT and number theory, focusing on the moments of the Riemann zeta function and families of L-functions.

We examine the conjectural asymptotics of moments of the Riemann zeta function on the critical line and compare them with predictions from the characteristic polynomial of random unitary matrices. Specifically, we analyze the behavior of the \(2k\)-th moment: \[\tag{1} M_k(T) = \int_0^T |\zeta(1/2 + it)|^{2k} dt,\] and show its connection to random matrix eigenvalue statistics.

Theorem For \(k=1\), the leading order asymptotic of \(M_k(T)\) satisfies: \[\tag{2} M_1(T) \sim T \log T.\] For larger \(k\), conjectures suggest that: \[\tag{3} M_k(T) \sim c_k T (\log T)^{k^2},\] where \(c_k\) is a constant predicted by RMT.

Corollary The statistical behavior of moments of L-functions matches that of the characteristic polynomials of matrices from the Circular Unitary Ensemble (CUE), further strengthening the RMT-number theory correspondence.

We discuss the implications of these results for the study of the zeros of L-functions, connections to random matrix eigenvalue statistics, and possible refinements of moment conjectures.

The Distribution of Eigenvalues in Random Matrix Models and Number-Theoretic Implications

Tags: Random Matrices

FirstName LASTNAME Affiliation
FirstName LASTNAME Affiliation

Random matrix theory (RMT) has proven to be an essential tool in understanding fundamental problems in number theory, particularly in the study of the distribution of zeros of L-functions and related arithmetic objects. This talk will explore recent advances in the statistical properties of eigenvalues in random matrix ensembles and their implications for number theory.

We focus on the connection between the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE), Gaussian Unitary Ensemble (GUE), and the distribution of low-lying zeros of the Riemann zeta function and families of L-functions. In particular, we examine the local spacing statistics of these zeros and their resemblance to eigenvalues of large random matrices.

Theorem Let \(M_N\) be an \(N \times N\) random Hermitian matrix from the GUE. The scaled eigenvalue spacing distribution converges to the sine kernel: \[\tag{1} P(s) = \frac{\sin^2(\pi s)}{(\pi s)^2},\] which matches the pair correlation function of the normalized zeros of the Riemann zeta function on the critical line.

Corollary The statistical properties of low-lying zeros of L-functions in a given family follow the same universal laws as eigenvalues of random matrix ensembles, providing strong evidence for the random matrix conjectures in number theory.

We will also discuss the broader implications of these results in analytic number theory, including conjectures on the distribution of primes and higher moments of L-functions.
